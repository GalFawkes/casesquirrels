Mysterious Acorn Stashes found on CWRU campus - students and faculty are concerned, the Trees are not

CLEVELAND — In a shocking discovery made by students, large acorn stashes have been sighted across the greenspaces of Case Western Reserve University’s campus. In particular, piles of acorns have been found on the grassy areas which make up the Quad, the field outside of Veale Athletic Center, and the open space surrounding Leutner in North Residence Village. The piles have accumulated over the past week through the recent snowy weather.

        “Yeah, it was really weird,” said third-year student Tiffany Jacobson. She claims to have been walking to her physics lab when she caught sight of what she described as a “really big pile of acorns. Like, really big.” Jacobson also noted a few squirrels perched around the pile, and while it is not uncommon to see squirrels around the campus, these squirrels supposedly remained completely still despite her approaching the pile for a closer look. This is unusual since squirrels tend to be skittish creatures, even after years of being habituated to the presence of students and faculty on campus.

        CWRU administration advises students not to disturb these piles until they are advised by animal control on how to proceed. In a recent university-wide email, they advised students to “avoid climbing in or approaching a Tree, grabbing leaves or acorns from a Tree, disrupting a squirrel in a Tree, and otherwise interacting with a Tree” until the matter is resolved. All questions should be directed to the university.
